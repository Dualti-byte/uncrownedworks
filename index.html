The Black Gallery

"Art does not reproduce what is visible, but makes visible what is not always visible." (Paul Klee)

The general objective of the project is to focus on the isolation of people with visual impairments, In particular to encourage reflection on the common conception of aesthetics. The project was carried out through the formation of a creative laboratory made up of people with visual impairments, who were asked to develop the theme: ‘’ How do I see you? ’'. The second activity proposed was the exhibition of the results of the laboratory within the ''Multisensory path in the dark'' organized by the U.N.I.Vo.C. (Italian National Union of Volunteers pro Blind) during the ''Fiera dei Beni Comuni''(Fair of Common Goods) organized by CSV Naples at the Domenico Martuscelli Institute in Naples. The Black Gallery is a gallery completely in the dark, an exhibition that allows the viewer to interact with the artworks as it was for their creator. The Gallery, completely structured in the dark, with the guidance of blind people and laboratory workers, recorded the presence of 130 visitors on 14 and 15 December 2018.

Suspended Smile

«When a Neapolitan is happy for some reason, instead of paying for a single coffee, the one he would drink, he pays for two, one for himself and one for the customer who comes next. It is like offering a coffee to the rest of the world…» (Il Caffè Sospeso, Luciano De Crescenzo)

From the Creative Laboratory of ForcellaStreet comes Sorriso Sospeso, inspired by the well-established Neapolitan tradition of "suspended coffee", which consists of leaving a coffee drink paid at the bar for those in need. Re-proposing the dynamic of "suspended coffee" ,and, using the smile as the protagonist, the project starts from the streets of Naples, specifically from one of its poorest neighborhoods, which does not enjoy a good reputation. The idea is developed by making the Suspended Smiles usable through video pills accompanied by dedicated QR Codes scattered throughout the city of Naples. Starting from a custom, which is part of the Neapolitan culture and represents it, with the prospect of inspiring a collective custom through a web app that allows people to generate and donate their own Suspended Smile, with the intention of being able to collect and share Smiles from all over the world...
